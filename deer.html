DEER

Deer are graceful and adaptable mammals belonging to the family Cervidae. Their scientific name varies depending on the species, but one of the most well-known is the red deer (Cervus elaphus). These herbivorous animals are found in forests, grasslands, and mountainous regions across the world. Known for their antlers, which are typically found on males, deer play a crucial role in ecosystems by helping to shape vegetation and serving as prey for predators.

What Deer Eat (Herbivore Diet)

  • Leaves

  • Grass

  • Bark

  • Acorns

  • Fruits and berries

Deer are a diverse group of animals found all over the world. They come in different sizes, colors, and habitats, each with unique characteristics. The table below highlights some common deer species and their key features.

Species Size Habitat Special Feature
White-tailed Deer 3-4 ft tall Forests & grasslands White fur under tail
Red Deer 4-5 ft tall Europe & Asia Large branching antlers
Moose 6-7 ft tall North America & Scandinavia Largest deer species
Reindeer (Caribou) 3-4 ft tall Arctic regions Both males & females have antlers
Sika Deer 2-3 ft tall Japan & Eastern Asia Spotted coat in summer
